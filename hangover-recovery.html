The 4-Hour Hangover Recovery Plan

The goal is to reduce symptom intensity fast by doing the right basics in the right order: rehydrate + electrolytes, calm inflammation/irritation, then restore energy + sleep.

Not medical advice. If symptoms are severe (confusion, chest pain, fainting, persistent vomiting, blood), seek urgent medical care.

Timeline protocol

Use whichever parts you can tolerate. Don’t force food if you’re nauseous — prioritise fluids first.

Hour 0 — Stabilise
  • Water + electrolytes: 500–750ml over 30–60 minutes.
  • Light carbs: toast/banana/rice (small portions).
  • Reduce stimulation: dark room, low noise.

If you’re vomiting: take small sips frequently rather than chugging.

Hour 1 — Repair
  • Magnesium glycinate (optional, gentle form).
  • Breathing: 4–6 slow breaths/min for 3–5 minutes.
  • Light movement: short walk if it helps headache.

Avoid intense exercise — it can worsen dehydration.

Hour 2–4 — Restore
  • Balanced meal: carbs + protein (see food section).
  • Hydrate steadily: water + electrolytes in cycles.
  • Sleep: nap or early bedtime if possible.

Sleep is the unfair advantage. Don’t fight it.

Food rules (simple)

  • If nauseous: start with bland carbs (toast, crackers, banana).
  • When stable: add protein (eggs, yogurt, chicken).
  • Go easy on fats early if your stomach is sensitive.
  • Avoid: more alcohol, heavy spicy meals, large coffee on an empty stomach.

The point is comfort + fuel. You’re restoring energy and reducing irritation.

FAQ

Can I just drink coffee and power through?

Coffee can help alertness, but it won’t fix dehydration or electrolyte imbalance. If you use caffeine, pair it with water + electrolytes and food.

Why electrolytes instead of just water?

Hangovers often involve fluid + electrolyte imbalance. Electrolytes help your body retain fluids and may reduce headache/fatigue for some people.

When should I worry and get medical help?

If you have severe symptoms (confusion, fainting, chest pain, blood, uncontrolled vomiting, severe abdominal pain), seek urgent medical evaluation.
