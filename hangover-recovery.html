Recovery Guide

Hangover Recovery:
What Actually Helps

A hangover is not one condition. It is the convergence of several physiological processes — each addressable, none quickly. Understanding what is happening inside the body is the first step toward managing it sensibly.

01 — The Biology

What Is Actually Happening

A hangover is a systemic response triggered by alcohol metabolism and its byproducts — not simply the aftermath of intoxication. Five primary mechanisms drive the experience.

01 Dehydration Alcohol suppresses ADH, causing kidneys to excrete more fluid than consumed. Result: systemic dehydration.
02 Electrolyte Imbalance Increased urination depletes sodium, potassium, magnesium — contributing to headaches and fatigue.
03 Inflammation Alcohol triggers cytokine release — pro-inflammatory molecules associated with nausea and fatigue.
04 Blood Sugar Disruption Alcohol inhibits gluconeogenesis in liver. Result: hypoglycemia causing shakiness and weakness.
05 Sleep Fragmentation Alcohol suppresses REM sleep. Even full night produces non-restorative sleep, compounding fatigue.

02 — The Fundamental Limit

There Is No Instant Cure

No supplement, food, beverage, or practice will accelerate alcohol elimination from the body. The liver metabolizes ethanol at a fixed biological rate — approximately 0.015% BAC per hour. That rate cannot be meaningfully increased.

Recovery is a function of time, supported — not replaced — by sensible choices. What is possible is addressing secondary symptoms: dehydration, nutrient depletion, inflammation, and sleep debt.

03 — Evidence-Informed Guidance

Practical Recovery

The following approaches address identifiable mechanisms of a hangover. None eliminate it. All are consistent with widely accepted clinical guidance.

  • 01
    Rehydrate With Electrolytes

    Plain water addresses volume depletion but not electrolyte loss. Oral rehydration salts (ORS), electrolyte drinks, or coconut water restore sodium and potassium more effectively.

  • 02
    Eat Light, Simple Foods

    Eggs contain cysteine (supports acetaldehyde breakdown). Bananas replenish potassium. Clear broth restores sodium. Plain toast raises blood glucose without stressing stomach.

  • 03
    Ibuprofen for Headache — With Caution

    Ibuprofen is reasonable for hangover headache in healthy adults. Take with food. Do not exceed standard dosage. Avoid if you have gastric ulcers or kidney issues.

  • 04
    Avoid Acetaminophen / Paracetamol

    Acetaminophen is processed by same hepatic pathway as alcohol. When alcohol remains in system, even standard doses carry meaningful risk of liver toxicity.

  • 05
    Rest

    Sleep debt from alcohol-disrupted sleep cannot be immediately repaid, but rest reduces inflammatory burden and allows metabolic clearance to continue.

04 — Common Misconceptions

What Does Not Work

Several widely practiced remedies persist despite absence of supporting evidence. Each addresses neither BAC nor the biological processes driving hangover symptoms.

Myth
"Coffee sobers you up."
False Caffeine is a stimulant that masks sedation. It does not alter BAC, accelerate liver metabolism, or restore cognitive function. It produces sensation of alertness while intoxication continues unchanged.
Myth
"A cold shower clears your head."
False Cold shower stimulates sympathetic nervous system, creating temporary alertness. Has no effect on blood alcohol concentration.
Myth
"Hair of the dog works."
False — and counterproductive Consuming alcohol to manage hangover delays symptoms by extending intoxication. Symptoms resume — and may intensify — once additional alcohol clears.

05 — Critical Safety Information

Recognizing Alcohol Poisoning

A hangover, however severe, is distinct from alcohol poisoning. Alcohol poisoning is a medical emergency. Confusing the two — or assuming a person will "sleep it off" — can be fatal.

If any of the following are present, call emergency services immediately. Do not wait.

Unconsciousness or unresponsiveness Seizures Slow or irregular breathing Fewer than 8 breaths per minute Blue-tinged or pale skin Vomiting while unconscious Extreme cold to touch Cannot be woken
Call emergency services immediately — 112 in India, 999 in UK, 911 in US.
Place person in recovery position. Stay with them until help arrives.
