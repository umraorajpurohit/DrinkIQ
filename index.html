Why most hangover advice fails

The internet mixes myths with half-truths. Most “tips” fail because they treat the symptoms randomly instead of targeting the core drivers in order.

  • Coffee can increase alertness, but may worsen dehydration and jitters.
  • Greasy food doesn’t “soak up alcohol” after the fact — it’s mostly comfort.
  • Hair-of-the-dog delays recovery by adding more alcohol.
  • Random supplements without timing = expensive placebo.

This protocol focuses on: rehydration + electrolyte balance, inflammation reduction, and supporting recovery behaviors — in a simple sequence.

The timeline protocol

Use this like a checklist. Keep it simple and consistent.

Hour 0

Stabilise

  • 500–750ml water + electrolytes
  • Light carbs (toast, banana, rice)
  • Cool, dark room if sensitive
Hour 1

Repair

  • Magnesium (gentle form)
  • Rest + slow breathing
  • Very light movement if you can
Hour 2–4

Restore

  • Balanced meal (carbs + protein)
  • Continue hydration steadily
  • Short nap or early sleep

FAQ

Does coffee help a hangover?

It can increase alertness, but it may worsen dehydration, anxiety, and stomach irritation for some people. If you use it, pair it with water + electrolytes.

How long does a hangover last?

Commonly 8–24 hours, depending on alcohol amount, sleep, hydration, and individual sensitivity. A structured protocol can reduce symptom intensity and duration.

Is IV therapy worth it?

For severe dehydration it can help, but most people do well with oral rehydration + electrolytes, rest, and food. If you have severe symptoms, seek medical advice.
