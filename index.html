Frequently Asked Questions

The legal Blood Alcohol Content (BAC) limit for driving in India is 0.03% (30mg per 100ml of blood) under the Motor Vehicles Act. Driving above this limit is a criminal offence and can result in fines, imprisonment, or licence suspension. However, impairment begins at even lower levels — the safest choice is always to not drive after consuming any alcohol.

This varies significantly by body weight, biological sex, food intake, and individual metabolism. As a very rough estimate, a 70kg adult male who has not eaten may approach 0.03% BAC after approximately 1–2 standard 30ml pegs of whisky (42.8% ABV) consumed over an hour. Women and lighter individuals will reach the same BAC with less alcohol. Use our free calculator above for a personalised estimate.

The average liver processes alcohol at approximately 0.015% BAC per hour. So if your estimated peak BAC is 0.09%, it will take roughly 6 hours to fully clear. This rate is largely fixed — drinking water, eating food, exercising, or drinking coffee after the fact does not significantly accelerate it. Only time reduces BAC.

Evidence supports: electrolyte rehydration (ORS sachets, coconut water — not just plain water), light foods (eggs, bananas, toast, clear broth), ibuprofen for headache (taken with food — avoid paracetamol/Crocin which stresses the liver), and rest. Coffee, cold showers, and "hair of the dog" do not help and may make things worse. See our full Hangover Recovery Guide.

DrinkIQ uses the Widmark equation — the established scientific formula for estimating BAC — but these are estimates only, not measurements. Real BAC is affected by factors like exact food consumption, hydration, medications, liver health, body fat percentage, and the precise alcohol content of each drink. Results can differ significantly from a breathalyser reading. Always treat results as rough guidance only, never as grounds for deciding to drive.
